The First 90 Days by Michael Watkins

The first 90 days in a new leadership role are critical for setting the direction and tone for your tenure in the role.

To be successful in the first 90 days, you need to:

  • Clarify your goals and priorities
  • Build relationships and networks
  • Assess your team and organization
  • Communicate effectively
  • Take charge of your learning

To clarify your goals and priorities, you should:

  • Identify the most important challenges and opportunities facing your organization
  • Determine your key success factors and how you will measure progress
  • Set clear and measurable goals

To build relationships and networks, you should:

  • Identify key stakeholders and engage with them early on
  • Seek out mentors and advisors who can provide guidance and support
  • Build relationships with your team and colleagues

To assess your team and organization, you should:

  • Gather as much information as possible about the team and organization
  • Identify strengths and weaknesses, and develop a plan for addressing them
  • Assess the culture of the organization and determine how to align with it or change it as needed

To communicate effectively, you should:

  • Clearly articulate your vision and goals
  • Build trust and credibility through your actions and words
  • Use various communication channels to reach different stakeholders

To take charge of your learning, you should:

  • Identify areas where you need to improve your skills and knowledge
  • Seek out learning opportunities and resources
  • Reflect on your experiences and learning to continuously improve your performance

To ensure success in the first 90 days, you should also be proactive, adaptable, and resilient.
