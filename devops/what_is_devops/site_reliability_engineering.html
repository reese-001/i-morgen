What Is SRE?

  • Why is it called SRE?
    • Site Reliability Engineering (SRE) is called so because its primary focus is on the reliability, availability, and performance of a website, application, or service
    • The term "site" in this context refers to any system, application, or service that users interact with or depend on
    • How can we define "site"?
      • Large scale
      • Distributed environment
      • Some kind of web service
    • What is the significance of the word "reliability"?

SRE vs DevOps

  • Background
    • There are two general paths for implementing operational support in a DevOps environment
    • Note that it is typical that organizations will follow a hybrid approach that best fits their culture and resources
    • Pure DevOps approach
      • In this approach, the dev and ops teams are combined into a single team and work towards the common devops goals, such as
        • Continuous integration
        • Continuous delivery
        • Infrastructure automation
      • This team is responsible for both building and operating the software
    • SRE-driven approach
      • In this approach, a dedicated SRE team is created to focus on running and maintaining the software
      • This enables the development team remains focused on creating new features
      • The SRE team must remain in close contact with the dev team to ensure reliability, scalability and performance goals are met
      • This approach still follows the DevOps principles, such as
        • Collaboration
        • Shared Responsibility
        • Automation
    • Does having two separate teams risk reintroducing the issues that led to the creation of the DevOps movement?
      • Yes, that risk does exist - specifically the division between teams and the creation of silos
      • This risk can be reduced by the following practices
        • Shared goals and objectives
        • Regular communication
        • Collaboration on projects
        • Cross-functional teams
        • Blameless culture
        • Continuous learning and improvement
  • Characteristics of SRE
    • SRE is a discipline that combines software engineering and operations to build reliable, scalable and efficient applications
    • Work typically involves monitoring, alerting, incident management, post mortem analysis and implementing automations to minimize manual intervention
    • Typically have a strong background in software engineering and systems administration
    • Tooling includes Prometheus, Grafana, PagerDuty, etc
  • Characteristics of DevOps
    • Work on improving the collaboration between development and operations teams
    • Work typically involves automation, enabling faster and more reliable software releases
    • Must have a strong background in software development and IT operations
    • Tooling includes Jenkins, Docker, Kubernetes, and Ansible
