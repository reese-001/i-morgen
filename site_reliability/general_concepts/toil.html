Toil

Contents

  • Overview

    • Overview

    • Toil is work that is tied to running a production service
    • Charactoristics include
      • Manual
      • Repetitive
      • Automatable
      • Tactical
      • Devoid of enduring value
      • Scales linearly as a service grows
    • A rule of thumb is to keep toil to less then 50% of overall workload
      • Focus on the goal and resist pressures to compromise. Do not devolve into an ops team
    • Why not target 0% toil?
      • Toil can allow for quick wins and a distraction from the more mentally challenging engineering work
      • Eliminating toil is not possible due in part to
        • Dynamic environments
          • Constantly changing systems will produce a constant stream of new incidents
        • Unpredictable incidents
          • It is not feasible to eliminate all incidents and these incidents may require manual intervention
        • Technical limitations
          • Automation is not always feasible - for example humans may be needed to understand/route complex incidents
        • Resource constraints
          • Eliminating toil requires money, and money is always a constraint
        • Resistance to change
          • There may be limitations on what humans are willing to automate - for a variety of reasons
    • Career stagnation
      • Career progress stalls when you are not actively engaged in projects
    • Low morale
      • Thresholds will vary, but everyone has a limit to the amount of toil they can accept. Breach that threshold and burnout, boredom and discontent occur
    • Low productivity
      • When toil is high, engineers will spend less time on engineering work
