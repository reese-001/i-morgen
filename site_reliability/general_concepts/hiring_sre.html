    • Overview

    • Technical skills
      • Consider candidates who have strong technical skills in areas such as system administration, network design, cloud and software development
    • Experience with devops methodologies
      • CI/CD, IaC, monitoring, logging
    • Problem solving
      • Look for candidates who have a strong track record of solving challenging technical issues, and who are able to think creatively and outside the box to find solutions
    • Communication skills
      • Look for candidates who are articulate and able to explain technical concepts to non-technical people
    • Availability and on-call responsibilites
      • Consider the candidates' availability and willingness to be on-call when making your hiring decision
    • Cultural fit
      • It's important to ensure that the candidate fits well with the company's culture and values, as well as with the team they will be working with
    • Attitude and approach
      • Look for candidates who have a positive attitude, are open-minded and collaborative, and who are constantly seeking to learn and improve

    Onboarding

    • One way to approach onboarding new SRE's is to train towards being on call
      • "What does a new hire need to learn to go on call?"
      • How can my existing team of on-callers assess the readiness of the new hire for on-call?
    • Important to remember that not everyone has the same learning style
    • Recommended Training Practices (from Google)

      • Recommended Pattern    Anti Pattern
      Design concrete and sequential learning experiences for students to follow Deluging students with menial work (alert, ticket triage)
      Encourage reverse engineering, statistical thinking, working from foundational principles Training strictly through operator procedures, checklists, and playbooks
      Celebrate the analysis of failure by suggesting postmortums for students to read Treating outages as secrets to be buried in order to avoid blame
      Creating contained but realistic breakages for students to fix using real monitoring and tooling Having the first opportunity to fix something already occur after a student is already on-call
      Role playing theoretical disaters as a group, to intermingle a team's problem solving approaches Create experts on the team whose techniques and knowledge are compartimentalized
