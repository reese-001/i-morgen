Operational Excellence Pillar - AWS Well-Architected Framework

Overview

  • Intent
    • The operational excellence pillar includes how your organization supports your business objectives, your ability to run workloads effectively, gain insight into their operations, and to continuously improve supporting processes and procedures to deliver business value
    • intended to help organizations improve the reliability, availability, and scalability of their systems, while also reducing costs and minimizing the risk of disruptions to their operations

  • Design Principles
    • Perform operations as code
      • Apply the same engineering discipline that you use for application code to your entire environment
      • A practice of automating and scripting the tasks involved in operating and maintaining systems and infrastructure through the use of programming or scripting languages, this enables organizations to improve the efficiency, consistency, and reliability of their operations
    • Make frequent, small, reversible changes
    • Refine operations procedures frequently
      • Increased agility
      • Reduced risk
      • Improved collaboration
      • Easier testing and validation
    • Anticipate failure
      • Risk assessment
      • Regular system monitoring
      • Load testing
      • Regular backups
      • Maintenance and upgrades
      • Incident management
      • Ops as Code
        • Refers to the practice of automating and scripting the tasks involved in operating and maintaining systems and infrastructure.
        • Infrastructure as code (IAC)
        • Configuration management
        • CI/CD
        • Monitoring/Logging
        • Automated incident response
    • Learn from all operational failures

Organization

  • To set the priorities that will enable success, your teams need to have a shared understanding of ..
    • Your entire workload
    • Their role in it
    • Shared business goals
  • Best practices
    • Evaluate external customer needs
    • Involve key stakeholders to determine where to focus efforts on external customer needs
      • Personal: How have I done this
        • Incident Prioritization Framework
        • Customer Lifetime Value index
        • Operational infrastructure monitoring
        • Intelligent Incident Analysis
    • Anti patterns
      • You decided to not have customer support outside of core hours but have not reviewed the data nor understand how it will impact your customers
      • You develop a new feature without engaging customers or stakeholders
  • Evaluate internal customer needs
    • Similar to evaluating external customer needs, ensure you take input from business, development and ops teams when determining where to focus your efforts
      • Personal: How have I done this
        • Escalation process
        • Continued effort to increase visibility to core metrics
    • Anti patterns
      • Decide to reallocate your IP addresses without discussing with stakeholders and without knowledge of possible impact
      • Develop a new tool without consulting stakeholders to determine its value
